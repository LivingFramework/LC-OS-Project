# Example: Research Paper

**Domain:** Academic research collaboration  
**Use case:** PhD researcher working with AI to write a journal paper on climate adaptation

This example shows how a researcher uses LC-OS governance to manage a 6-month
paper-writing collaboration with an AI, preventing citation errors, scope drift,
and argument inconsistency.

## What's in this folder

| File | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| `strategy-master.md` | Research scope, methodology boundaries, academic standards |
| `running-document.md` | Current draft state, active arguments, open questions |
| `canonical-numbers.md` | All statistics, citations, and model outputs used in the paper |
| `failure-log.md` | Errors caught during the research and writing process |

## What makes research a high-risk AI collaboration

Research is uniquely vulnerable to AI failure modes:
- AI will confidently invent citations that don't exist
- AI will subtly shift terminology across sections
- AI will "round" or simplify numerical findings
- AI will drift from your methodological choices without flagging it

LC-OS addresses all four. See the failure log for real examples.
